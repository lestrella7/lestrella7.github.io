Who I Am & What I Do

I'm a Computer Science student at Georgia Institute of Technology graduating in May 2026, concentrating in Cybersecurity/Privacy and the People & Human Interaction threads. My interest in security goes beyond exploits and toolchains — I care about the human dimension of it: why people fall for attacks, how organizations build security culture, and what it means to design systems that protect real people.

Outside of coursework, I've spent four years teaching programming and security principles to students ranging from middle schoolers to high schoolers, leading community organizations, and competing in hardware security challenges. I'm drawn to work where technical rigor and communication skills are both required.

When I'm not debugging firmware or writing curriculum, I'm directing outreach efforts at Gracias ATL and advocating for Latine students in STEM through the Latin American Student Association.

Competed in the CSAW ESC using a ChipWhisperer-Nano to perform fault injection and side-channel analysis against embedded firmware.
