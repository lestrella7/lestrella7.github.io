About

Security is technical.
People make it complicated.

I'm a Computer Science student at Georgia Institute of Technology graduating May 2026, concentrating in Cybersecurity/Privacy and the People thread. My interest in security doesn't stop at the technical layer — I care about the human dimension of it: why people fall for attacks, how organizations build security culture, and what it means to design systems that protect real people.

Outside of coursework, I've spent years teaching programming and security principles to middle and high school students through Georgia Tech's Office of Outreach and EXCEL programs — from afterschool coding to engineering drone camps. I'm drawn to work where both technical depth and the ability to communicate clearly are required.

When I'm not reverse engineering firmware, I'm directing community outreach at Gracias ATL and advocating for Latine students in STEM.
